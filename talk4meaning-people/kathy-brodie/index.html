Kathy Brodie

Kathy Brodie is an Early Years Professional and Trainer based in East Cheshire, UK. She has worked in a variety of settings, including schools, and so knows what it feels like to be under pressure to deliver the best care and education for children, whilst juggling the daily necessities. However, she is enthusiastic that this is not only possible but is essential in order to raise the quality of provision that this country gives to its children in their Early Years. Kathy has a Masters in Early Childhood Education from the University of Sheffield and achieved her Diploma in Teaching Lifelong Learners (DTLLS) in 2013. The training that she provides is tailored to each nursery’s requirements so each course is unique. She believes training should be interactive and fun.

Typical courses

  • Observation, assessment and planning
  • Outdoor play
  • Understanding the EYFS
  • Sustained Shared Thinking
  • Effective Communication
  • Reflective practice

‘Observation, Assessment and Planning: Bringing it All Together‘ was published in 2013 and her second book ‘Sustained Shared Thinking‘ was published in 2014. Her next publication is due out in the Spring of 2015, which is a jointly edited book with Keith Savage entitled ‘Inclusion and Early Years Practice’.
